The Florida Insurance Problem

Palm Beach County Homeowners Are Getting Squeezed

Florida's insurance market is in crisis. Rates are up, carriers are pulling out, and most homeowners don't know their options. Here's what we see every day.

🌪

Hurricane Season Gets More Expensive Every Year

Windstorm premiums in coastal Palm Beach County have surged. Separate hurricane deductibles (5-10% of home value) catch homeowners off guard when they file a claim.

🌊

Flood Isn't Covered by Homeowners Insurance

80% of homeowners think standard insurance covers flooding. It doesn't. With Palm Beach County's flood zones and rising NFIP rates, a gap in coverage can be devastating.

🏠

Older Roofs Are Getting Denied Coverage

If your roof is 15+ years old, many Florida carriers won't write a policy. Some require expensive inspections. We work with carriers that cover older roofs without the runaround.

📈

Rates Up 50%+ Since 2020

Florida has seen the steepest insurance rate increases in the nation. Many homeowners are paying double what they paid five years ago for the same coverage.

🚪

Carriers Are Leaving Florida

Over a dozen insurers have exited the Florida market since 2020. If your carrier leaves, you could end up in the Citizens pool at higher rates with less coverage.

🤔

Citizens Is Not the Answer

Citizens Property Insurance is Florida's insurer of last resort — not your best option. Rates are often 25-40% higher than the private market, and coverage can be limited.

Common Questions

Florida Insurance FAQ

Answers to the questions Palm Beach County homeowners ask most.

Yes. Standard homeowners insurance does not cover flood damage — this surprises most homeowners. In Palm Beach County, many properties fall within FEMA-designated flood zones, making separate flood coverage essential. Both NFIP (National Flood Insurance Program) and private flood policies are available. An independent agent can compare both to find the best rate and coverage for your specific property.
Many Florida insurers limit coverage or significantly increase premiums for homes with roofs older than 15-20 years. Some carriers require a roof inspection or four-point inspection before they'll issue a policy. An independent agent can find carriers that work with older roofs and help you explore options like Florida's roof certification programs, which can extend your eligibility.
No. Citizens is Florida's insurer of last resort — it's designed as a safety net, not a first choice. Rates are often 25-40% higher than private market alternatives, and coverage can be more limited. An independent agency can search across 50+ carriers to find private market options that often provide better coverage at competitive rates. If you're currently with Citizens, it's worth getting a comparison.
Bundling home and auto insurance with the same carrier typically saves 15-25% on combined premiums. In Florida's high-rate market, this can translate to hundreds or even thousands of dollars in annual savings. An independent agent can identify the best bundle combinations across multiple carriers, which is something captive agents tied to a single company can't do.
A hurricane deductible is separate from your standard deductible and applies only to hurricane-related claims. In Florida, it's typically 2-10% of your home's insured value. For a home insured at $400,000, a 5% hurricane deductible means you'd pay $20,000 out of pocket before coverage kicks in. Understanding this number is critical for financial planning, and an agent can help you choose the right deductible level.
Windstorm insurance covers damage caused by hurricanes, tropical storms, and severe wind events. In coastal areas of Palm Beach County, windstorm coverage is essential and often required by mortgage lenders. Some homeowners policies include wind coverage, while others exclude it, requiring a separate policy. An independent agent can review your existing policy and make sure wind damage is covered.
Florida's insurance rates have increased over 50% since 2020 due to a combination of factors: increased hurricane frequency and severity, litigation abuse driving up insurer costs, reinsurance price spikes, and multiple carrier insolvencies. Legislative reforms in 2022 and 2023 aimed to stabilize the market, but rate relief has been slow. Working with an independent agent who shops dozens of carriers is one of the most effective ways to manage costs.
